### 5. Structured Data (Schema Markup)

**What It Is**: Special code that tells Google exactly what your business is and what you offer

**What We Added**:

#### Organization Schema
- Tells Google: "This is Monster Streams, an IPTV company"
- Includes: Name, logo, contact information, service area
- **Benefit**: Enables rich snippets and Knowledge Graph eligibility

#### Service Schema
- Tells Google: "This company offers IPTV streaming services"
- Includes: Service type, description, area served (Phuket, Thailand, Worldwide)
- **Benefit**: Helps Google understand your service offering

#### Contact Schema
- Tells Google: "Here's how to contact this business"
- Includes: Email addresses, service areas, languages
- **Benefit**: Enables contact information in search results

#### Pricing Schema
- Tells Google: "Here are the pricing options"
- Includes: Price range ($15-$120), currency, availability
- **Benefit**: Price may appear in search results

**Why This Matters**: 
- ‚úÖ **Rich Snippets**: Your search results can show extra information (ratings, prices, etc.)
- ‚úÖ **Knowledge Graph**: Potential to appear in Google's knowledge panel
- ‚úÖ **Better Understanding**: Google understands your business better
- ‚úÖ **Competitive Advantage**: Many competitors don't use structured data

**Real-World Impact**: Rich snippets can increase click-through rates by 30-50% compared to regular listings.

## üéì Part 10: Understanding SEO Jargon

### Common SEO Terms Explained

**Title Tag**: The blue headline in Google search results  
**Meta Description**: The text snippet under the title  
**H1/H2/H3**: Heading tags that structure your content  
**Keywords**: Words people search for  
**Schema Markup**: Code that tells Google about your business  
**Rich Snippets**: Enhanced search results with extra information  
**Mobile-First**: Google primarily uses mobile version for rankings  
**Core Web Vitals**: Google's speed and user experience metrics  
**Organic Traffic**: Visitors from search engines (free, not paid ads)  
**Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: Percentage of people who click your result  
**Bounce Rate**: Percentage of visitors who leave immediately  
**Local SEO**: Optimizing for local/geographic searches
